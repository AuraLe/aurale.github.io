Understanding SEO and the Customers

On 9 Feb 2017

Understanding SEO

SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. It assesses current on and off webpage factors, then tries to improve them so that search engines can understand the website's' or webpage's structure better. That way, the site's ranking will get boosted.

Basically, a site with higher traffic will eventually climb up in rank. There are many ways to increase the traffic: Links through social media or bigger sites, relevant site contents, etc.

On-page factor: literally page contents, appearance and page’s structure, which are easily controlled by developers. Off-page factore: basically outside factors that you can’t directly control. (Example: your site’s link on other websites or social media). On-page factors can only be tweaked to a certain level, while off-page factors are more important and don’t have such limit.

Web developers should prioritize users’ experience over rankings because user preference is the main factor that can boost site ranking. Easy-to-use-and-understand sites mean users will stay on site longer, leading to better ranking.

For ecommerce sites, there are 5 buying processes, ranging from when customers start to search for the solution to their need, to after they’ve done purchasing a product. SEO can be implemented in any of the processes, and depending on the site’s keywords, it can help boosting potential sources of traffic.

Quality content, good usability, and external links provide better chance at higher ranking. Target a specific group of users, have more links on other places that lead to the site, pay for ad on big sites, etc. SEO is an on going process that takes time to come into fruition.

Understanding the Customers

Do “Target market research” to learn about customers’ trend so that you can shape the website according to research result to make it more appealing for customers. It will also save money and time on SEO.

Create a "Buyer Persona" - a persona with the info collected from customers research - and let it be a targeted customer. Then, try to build website that respond to his/her needs and preferences.

Short, simple keywords have a high search volume, but more competition. Longer keywords have lesser search volume, but are more specific. A good SEO strategy will include both types of keywords.

Gather potential keywords that customers may search (can use Google to compare result). Use Google AdWords to expand the list, as a keyword planner. Good keyword and deciding target market are key components to a quality website. Short and long-tail keywords will ensure more exposure for the site.
